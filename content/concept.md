# Concept

The Interactive Signalling Laboratory (SigLab) is an educational toolkit that teaches railway signalling with physical components, constrained movement, and collaborative operation tasks.

## Learning goals

- Understand signalling logic through direct interaction.
- Practice block conditions and route dependencies.
- Build system-thinking skills around safety constraints.
- Learn with cooperative, scenario-based sessions.

## What makes SigLab different

- Physical model and game flow instead of slide-only instruction.
- Discrete movement and timing that keep operational effects visible.
- Progressive complexity from starter sessions to advanced tasks.

## Target audiences

- Railway engineering students
- Vocational and professional railway training
- Higher-education teaching teams
- Museums and outreach contexts

## Versions

- Paper-focused prototyping workflows
- Wooden and 3D-printed durable setup
